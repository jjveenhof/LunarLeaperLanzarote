# La Corona LiDAR -- georeferencing & re-alignment record

Reproducibility record of EVERY transform applied to Tomasi's source LiDAR to produce
the georeferenced, re-registered clouds used in the thesis (gravity cross-sections,
depth maps, 3-D plot). Read top-to-bottom = the data's journey, in chronological order.

- CRS throughout: EPSG:4083 (REGCAN95 / UTM zone 28N), orthometric heights.
- Tools: CloudCompare (manual + ICP registration, rasterizing); Python for verification
  (verify_alignment.py), transform recovery (recover_transform.py), slicing (slice_tube.py).
- Sub-clouds are referred to by their `Original cloud index` (idx0..idx6) and the working
  names we gave them. CloudCompare display COLOURS are arbitrary (they change with user
  settings) and are NOT used here. The only colour convention is in the PLOTS:
  blue = reference, yellow = bridge, green = tube/mover.

## Reading the transforms (frames & rotation reference)

Every 4x4 acts as  **p' = R.p + t**  (rotate by R, then translate by t). The rotation R
is about the **ORIGIN (0,0,0) of the frame the matrix lives in -- NOT about the cloud.**
So the "centre of rotation" is wherever (0,0,0) sits in that frame:

- Junction matrices (sec. 2) are in CloudCompare's SHIFTED-LOCAL frame (global shift
  -651100, -3227000, 0); its origin (0,0,0) sits at true (651100, 3227000), a few hundred
  m from the junction -> translation columns are modest (tens-hundreds of m).
- Jameo de la Gente matrices (sec. 4) are in TRUE EPSG:4083; its origin is the UTM false
  origin, ~thousands of km away. Rotating the cloud about that far origin sweeps it
  enormously, so the translation column is huge (~15 km) -- pure bookkeeping that cancels
  the sweep, NOT a physical move.

Therefore do NOT read the translation column as "how far the cloud moved." The
frame-independent, physically meaningful quantities are (1) the **rotation angle** and
(2) the **centroid displacement** (where the cloud's centre actually goes). Any rigid move
= "rotate about the centroid by that angle, then shift the centroid by that vector" -- that
is what the sec. 4a "real move (centroid)" + "rotation about centroid" lines report, and it
is how to sanity-check a matrix (angle in deg, move in metres).

- **Apply / verify** a matrix: CloudCompare > Edit > Apply Transformation (paste the 4x4).
  To check a matrix makes sense, apply it to the ORIGINAL cloud and confirm it lands on the
  FINAL export.
- **Global shift**: stored_local = true + shift. Editing a cloud's global shift in CC
  relabels the shift WITHOUT moving the true coordinates (verified).
- **Net 4x4 recovery** from before/after exports: recover_transform.py (Kabsch on
  scalar-field-matched points) -- frame-safe, sidesteps CC's mixed intermediate frames.

## 0. Source data

Tomasi's merged point cloud `LaCorona.bin`, carrying an `Original cloud index` scalar
field (idx0..idx6; split via Edit > Scalar fields > Filter By Value). Relevant subsets:
- idx0 = SE passage at Puerta Falsa -- trusted / correct orientation (the reference).
- idx1 = big NW passage at Puerta Falsa -- misaligned (swung anticlockwise).
- idx2 = junction patch at Puerta Falsa -- the "bandaid" bridge between idx0 and idx1.
- idx5 = tunnel at Jameo de la Gente (~870 m NW).
- idx6 = jameo + surface at Jameo de la Gente.

The cloud has NO valid georeferencing as delivered, and is internally inconsistent
(scan sections misaligned by metres in XY, plus local Z offsets and tilts).

## 1. Manual pre-shift to basemap (pre-record)

Whole cloud translated by eye to fit the jameos onto a Google satellite basemap:
**dE = -1100 m, dN = +6900 m** (horizontal only). Gets the cave roughly onto the map;
leaves a residual few-metre offset, worse near the jameos (surface points past the rim
bias the fit). Refined to RTK truth in stage 3.

## 2. Puerta Falsa junction re-registration (2026-06-15)  [feeds Line 3]

The scans meeting at the fieldwork junction were misaligned. Working on a copy
(`LaCorona_aligning.bin`), with idx0 (SE passage) as the fixed reference, the two
misaligned scans were re-registered in sequence: idx2 -> idx0, then idx1 -> moved idx2.
Method: coarse by eye, then Z-locked ICP (fine). Crop "Cave around Puerta Falsa".
Matrices are in CloudCompare's shifted-local frame (global shift -651100, -3227000, 0),
i.e. the stage-1 pre-shift frame -- BEFORE the stage-3 RTK correction.

### 2c. Verification (2026-06-16, verify_alignment.py)

Aligned subsets exported to `Reregistered clouds/` (PF_ref_after / PF_stitch_after /
PF_tube_after.txt, true EPSG:4083). Independent NN residuals + figure `alignment_check.png`:
- idx1->idx2: ~20% of idx1 pts (the true overlap) within 0.3 m, mean NN 0.006 m -> mm fit.
- idx2->idx0: closest ~0.25-0.5 m = idx0's sparsity floor -> as good as data allows.
- idx0 + idx2 + idx1 form one continuous passage; the tube cross-section rings coincide;
  the idx0 ceiling line is level -> no residual tilt. Z-rotation + horizontal translation
  model confirmed sufficient. (Baseline pre-alignment idx2->idx0 was ~mean 8.7 / med 5.6 m.)

## 3. Puerta Falsa RTK georef correction (2026-06-16)  [absolute anchor]

Pins the (already junction-corrected) cave to cm-accurate RTK GNSS truth at the Puerta
Falsa jameo. RTK rim trace (25 pts, `Line=Edge`) + 1 plumb tie, from
`Data/GNSS/Cleaned/CleanedGNSS_GPR_FlowerPetals.csv` -> `PuertaFalsa_edge_RTK.xyz`,
`_plumb_RTK.xyz`. The plumb pick on the cloud was 9.17 m E / 1.27 m S of RTK truth.

TRANSLATION ONLY (no rotation -- a ~20 m rim arc cannot constrain rotation over a 6-7 km

0 1 0   1.269999980927
0 0 1   0.000000000000
0 0 0   1.000000000000

## 4. Jameo de la Gente local re-georeference (2026-06-30)  [feeds Line 5]

A second, independent local anchor ~870 m NW, for the Line 5 gravity cross-section. No
cm-RTK rim survey here, so lower precision than Puerta Falsa. Bridge-cloud pattern:
drone (truth-ish) <- idx6 (jameo+surface, bridge) <- idx5 (tunnel, mover).

**Truth hierarchy:**
- RTK GNSS lines L5 (N-S) + L2 (NE-SW), cm-accurate -> absolute datum (but sparse, lines
  only). `Gente_rtk_L5.xyz`, `Gente_rtk_L2.xyz`.
- Drone topo -> correct shape + horizontal, but a local ~+0.35 m vertical bias vs RTK
  (uniform). So: drone shape, RTK datum. (Bias is LOCAL -- Puerta Falsa drone was fine.)
- LiDAR (idx5, idx6) -> cave geometry only; here it is off ~7 m in XY, plus a real
  W-high/E-low tilt and spurious curled edges on idx6.

**Method:** idx6 -> full-rotation ICP to the drone (NOT Z-locked, to tip out the tilt) on
the trimmed core, then a -0.35 m datum drop onto RTK-true elevation. idx5 -> ICP to the
corrected idx6 on the small pit overlap. The -0.35 m drop was applied to the cave + the
local drone crop (C2C drone-vs-RTK Z-component was -0.35 m; the full 0.84 m C2C gap is
mostly drone sparsity).

### 4a. Net 4x4 per cloud (recovered frame-safe)

via their preserved scalar-field signatures (recover_transform.py; exports in

### 4b. Coregistration check (2026-07-01, verify_alignment.py --gente)

Independent NN residuals on the corrected exports (idx5 + idx6 + drone + RTK L5/L2).
Figure `gente_check.png` (plot colours: blue = drone/reference, yellow = idx6/bridge,
green = idx5/mover). NN residuals (floored by the sparser cloud's spacing), not
rigid-fit RMS:
- **idx5 <-> idx6** (internal, pit throat): within 0.5 m, mean 0.26 m / RMS 0.29 m,
  p5 0.17 m -> the two LiDAR scans agree at the skylight.
- **idx6 <-> drone** (surface fit): drone->idx6 (dense ref = real separation) within 1 m
  RMS 0.30 m, median 0.17 m. Reverse idx6->drone 0.67-0.88 m is drone-sparsity-floored
  (the direction the ICP reported, ~0.95 m). idx6 sits on the drone to ~0.3 m.
- **drone -> RTK** (absolute datum): RTK L5 p5 0.21 m, L2 p5 0.24 m; sub-metre throughout,
  at the drone's ~2 m floor. Confirms the -0.35 m drop -- surface on RTK, tunnel beneath.

Conclusion: validated by three independent datasets (internal LiDAR pit agreement, drone
surface fit, RTK datum). Lower precision than Puerta Falsa, but area is the gravity metric
and <0.5 m vertical is datum choice, so ample.

## 6. Scope & limitations (do NOT re-open for the thesis)

- A single rigid translation CANNOT align all jameos -- the source LiDAR is internally
  inconsistent. After anchoring to RTK at Puerta Falsa, Jameo de la Gente sat ~5-6 m too
  far west BEFORE its local re-georef; that is the LiDAR's own coregistration error, not
  the basemap (Google imagery is smooth over ~1 km and RTK+drone confirm it at Puerta
  Falsa). The two measured sites are locally exact; the tube between/beyond is approximate.
- Do NOT least-squares-balance the whole tube -- that would degrade the RTK-validated
  anchor for cosmetics elsewhere. The dataset has several OTHER internal misalignments away
  from the measurement sites; left unfixed (out of scope, no bearing on the gravity/GPR
  lines). Possible future summer project: re-register the full tube end-to-end.
- The single merged whole-cave deliverable is DROPPED (decision 2026-07-01): the cave is
  consumed piecewise and a merged product would only be approximate along the tube.
